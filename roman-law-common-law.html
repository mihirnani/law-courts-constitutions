Roman Law and the
English Common Law

A comparison of two long, changing legal traditions: not code against cases, and not two sealed worlds. The useful questions concern sources, remedies, property, obligation, status, legal reasoning and the institutions that made doctrine possible.

Scope. The Roman side concentrates on classical juristic law and Justinian’s compilation. The English side begins with the central royal courts of the twelfth century and follows common law, equity and later reform. Short extracts are linked to full primary texts. Historical comparison is not legal advice.
