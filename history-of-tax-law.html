A History
of Tax Law

Tax law developed where public power met private property, accounting, territorial sovereignty, and administrative information. Its history is a movement from levies and consent to income measurement, treaty allocation, anti-avoidance, and global coordination.

Scope. This is a selective comparative timeline, not a universal history. Its main threads are the English fiscal constitution, modern income taxation, Indian direct-tax development, and the international system that produced transfer pricing, BEPS, Pillars One and Two, and the UN tax-convention process. Current-status entries were reviewed in June 2026. This is not tax or legal advice.
